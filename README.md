# rov-brake-system

Summary

Manually operated three-stage mechanical brake system designed for an ROV winch in offshore wind operations. Features a gear-and-pawl locking mechanism, Kevlar-lined stainless steel shoes, and multi-linkage actuation. Completed at Rovtech Solutions Ltd, Aberdeen.

Constraints & Limitations

Budget: £250–£350
Frame envelope: 550 mm internal width, side-mounting footprint
Clamping force sufficient for tether drag control; does not meet the 900–1,400 Nm target for full ROV static holding under worst-case conditions
Physical testing was limited due to fabrication time constraints; validation conducted via analytical force modelling


Tools & Methods

SolidWorks (CAD modelling and assembly)
Analytical force transmission modelling
Material selection for marine environments
Fastener specification (M5–M14, torque-controlled)


Context
Completed as part of a mechanical engineering internship at Rovtech Solutions Ltd (an Acteon company), working within a live offshore wind project environment. The brief was set by the project leader; design, analysis, and documentation were carried out independently.
